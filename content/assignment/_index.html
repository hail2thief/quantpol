Preparation

You will succeed in this class if you (1) attend lecture, (2) attend section, and (3) do the problem sets.

Weekly check-in

Every week, while you finish working through the content, I want to hear about what you learned and what questions you still have. To do this, you will fill out a short-response on Canvas that simply asks you what (if anything) you: 1) found interesting or compelling from the content so far; 2) found confusing or muddly from the content so far. TAs will compile and discuss the top issues in section.

Problem sets

To practice coding in R you will complete problem sets throughout the quarter. Feel free to work together on the problem sets but you must turn in your own answers to Canvas. You cannot work in groups of more than three people.

I’ll accept problem sets late one day (but you will lose a full letter-grade). Beyond that I won’t accept late problem sets.

Final project

The final project for this class will be an original analysis of data that you care about. The project must incorporate:

  1. A significant amount of data wrangling
  2. At least one visualization of the main idea in the project
  3. A DAG outlining barriers to causal identification
  4. Other statistics, figures, or details that flesh out the project

The primary deliverable will be:

  1. a Powerpoint slide summarizing and visualizing the main idea in your project
  2. a short memo providing added detail to explain the project
  3. an R script to reproduce your analysis
